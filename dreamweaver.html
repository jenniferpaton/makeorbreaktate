Dreamweaver

"Weave a little dream with me!" The Dreamweavers project was a participatory installation that requires contribution from the visiting public.

"My friend did not want to come along with me today. He isn't one for arts and crafts. But once we sat down and started working on the patches, he fell silent and concentrated very hard on his work. In the end, he admitted he hadn't expected to enjoy this activity, and felt very accomplished after hanging his patch on the Dreamweaver."

Dreamweaver Participant
Visitors were provided with fabric patches and crafting materials with which they made their "dreams" come to life. They were then asked to hang their "dreams" onto the Dreamweaver.

The activity addressed dreams and their effects on our waking lives, and encouraged participants to open up about their dreams. It also challenged participants to be creative about expressing how they believe their dreams would look like if manifested through physical materials.
This activity strived to make audiences comfortable with sharing certain feelings and memories in a public space fostering creativity, self-reflection, empathy, and inclusivity.

Evaluation

We believe the workshop yielded positive results and that the workshop objectives were largely met. Rather than building high visitor numbers, we wanted to provide a relaxing environment for participants and a space for productive creativity and self-reflection.

"One of the men made two patches - one for hanging on the Dreamweaver, and one to take home as a memory of his being a fighter in the War. We hadn't known he was in the War until today!... This activity shows how powerful arts and crafts can be in surfacing precious memories and eliciting strong emotional connections."

Creative Minds
The activity welcomed a moderate flow of visitors, especially in the afternoons, with a high turnover rate. Visitors easily understood the purpose of the workshop, and were enthusiastic about upcycling ordinary materials into something of artistic value. Visitors of a wide age range proved to be confident in manipulating the provided materials. Our collaborators at Creative Minds have graciously received the finished Dreamweavers artwork as a gift, and have displayed it at the Appleby House care home.
